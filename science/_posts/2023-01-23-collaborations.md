A project on finding high-redshift quasars in the southern hemisphere is in collaboration with the [4MOST ChANGES](https://www.eso.org/sci/meetings/2020/4MOST2/4MOST_CHANGES.pdf) survey. A project to study the CGM around high-z quasars with integral-field spectrographs (IFUs) brings together collaborators from [Gemini Observatory North](https://www.gemini.edu/), the [Max Planck Institute for Astronomy](https://www.mpia.de/) in Heidelberg, and the [Jet Propulsion Laboratory](https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/). I am also part of big upcoming surveys such as [LSST](https://www.lsst.org/), through the [LSST AGN Science Collaboration](https://agn.science.lsst.org/sites/default/files/LSST_AGN_SC_Roadmap_260722.pdf) (check the LSST AGN SC roadmap).

Despite my collaborations, I am also a member of [La Sociedad Chilena de Astronomía (SOCHIAS)](https://sochias.cl/) and [Centro de Astrofísica y Tecnologías Afines (CATA)](https://cata.cl/).
